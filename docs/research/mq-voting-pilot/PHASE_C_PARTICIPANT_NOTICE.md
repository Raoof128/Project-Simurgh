# Participant Notice — Simurgh Voting Pilot Phase C

**Version:** 2026-06-v1
**Pilot:** MQ Persian Society Voting Pilot — Phase C optional member pilot
**Status:** Draft — pending executive and ethics approval before distribution

---

## What is this?

You are invited to optionally take part in a research pilot called Project Simurgh. This pilot runs alongside the official MQ Persian Society election. It is completely optional.

**Your official vote is not affected whether you join or not.**

---

## What Simurgh does

Simurgh collects privacy-preserving session metadata to research whether digital voting sessions can be monitored for integrity without recording ballot content or personal information.

It records:

- A random session ID (not linked to your name or student ID)
- Whether you accepted or declined consent
- Timestamps for session start, submission, and completion
- Audit chain validity (proof that the session record was not tampered with)
- Focus-loss and paste event counts (how many times the browser window lost focus)

---

## What Simurgh does NOT collect

- Who you vote for or which candidate you select
- Your name, student ID, or email
- Screen recordings, screenshots, or webcam video
- Microphone or audio
- Clipboard contents or typed text
- App names, window titles, or process names
- Device serial numbers, MAC addresses, or precise device identifiers

---

## Your anonymous code

When you accept consent, Simurgh generates a random anonymous participant code for your session. Write it down if you want — it is shown once and not stored by Simurgh. It is not linked to your identity.

---

## Participation is voluntary

You can:

- **Decline** on the consent page — no data is collected and no session is created
- **Withdraw at any time** during the session — telemetry stops immediately and no report is produced
- **Participate fully** — your session metadata is used for aggregate research analysis only

There is no penalty for declining or withdrawing.

---

## How your data is used

Session data is used only for aggregate research analysis. No individual session result is used for any disciplinary purpose. The pilot does not decide election outcomes or flag individual voters.
