# Venue Requirement Audit

Audit date: 2026-06-24

## Primary Target: ACM AISec 2026

Official source: https://aisec.cc/

Verified requirements from official CFP:

- Deadline: 2026-07-24, 23:59 AoE.
- Submission site: `https://aisec26.hotcrp.com/`.
- Format: double-column ACM format; strict ACM compliance.
- Length: at most 10 pages excluding bibliography and well-marked appendices; at most 12 pages overall.
- Language: English.
- Anonymity: submissions must be properly anonymized. Author identity must not be leaked through code links, self-citations, headers, or repositories.
- Artifact: benchmark papers require a functional benchmark resource link at submission; code/artifact links must be anonymized when used.
- GenAI: official CFP requires a paragraph after references/appendices explaining Generative AI use.
- Desk-reject risks: non-anonymized PDF/artifact, format violations, page-limit violations, artifact-rule violations, missing GenAI statement.

Recommendation: Submit as original systems/security research with an anonymous artifact, not as a pure benchmark paper.

## Backup: NDSS 2027

Official source: https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2027/submissions/call-for-papers/

Verified from official page/search result:

- Fit: practical network/distributed systems security; topic scope includes security/privacy of AI/LLM-based systems.
- Fall deadline noted by public deadline trackers/search result: 2026-08-19 AoE, but final submission instructions need manual recheck before use.
- Artifact evaluation: NDSS has artifact processes, but exact 2027 details should be rechecked at submission time.

Recommendation: Strong backup if AISec timing fails, but paper would need NDSS formatting and a stronger systems evaluation narrative.

## Backup: USENIX Security 2027

Official source: https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity27

Verified requirements:

- Cycle 1 registration: 2026-08-18.
- Cycle 1 submission: 2026-08-25.

Related official source for Security 2026 policy/template: https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity26/call-for-papers

- Recent USENIX Security requirements include 13-page body, required Ethical Considerations appendix, and required Open Science appendix.

Recommendation: Strong but harder venue; requires a deeper evaluation and USENIX-specific ethics/open-science appendices.

## Backup: ACM CCS Workshop / CCS Rules

Official source: https://www.sigsac.org/ccs/CCS2026/call-for/call-for-papers.html

Relevant verified rules:

- Anonymous artifact URLs are required for online artifacts.
- Ethical Considerations appendix is mandatory for work with potential ethical concerns.

Recommendation: Useful formatting/ethics reference because AISec points to ACM/CCS-style formatting, but CCS main-paper timing is not the primary path.
